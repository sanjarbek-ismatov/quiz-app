# Business English Vocabulary Reference

Compiled from exam sample variants + extended coverage of common business English topics.

---

## 1. Management & Workplace Culture

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| unwritten rule | An unofficial norm in a workplace that is understood but never formally stated |
| management | The process of organizing and directing people and resources to achieve goals |
| promoted | Given a higher position or rank within an organization |
| misconduct | Unacceptable or improper behavior in a professional setting |
| due diligence | Careful investigation before signing a contract or making a business decision |
| on the spot | Immediately; also means being put in a difficult situation requiring instant response |
| flag (verb) | To mark or draw attention to something (e.g., "flag an issue") |
| wrap up | To finish or conclude a meeting, task, or project |
| stay put | To remain in one's current position without moving or changing |
| delegate | To assign responsibility or tasks to someone else |
| accountability | Being responsible for one's decisions and their consequences |
| appraisal | A formal evaluation of an employee's performance |
| grievance | A formal complaint made by an employee about workplace treatment |
| turnover | The rate at which employees leave and are replaced in a company |
| hierarchy | A system of ranks or authority levels within an organization |

---

## 2. Finance & Economics

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| cash flow | The movement of money in and out of a business over a period |
| profitability | The degree to which a business earns more money than it spends |
| salaries | Fixed regular payments made to employees, usually monthly |
| afford | To have enough money to pay for something |
| extravagant | Spending much more than necessary; excessive in cost |
| merger | When two companies combine to form a single new company |
| revenue | Total income generated by a business before expenses |
| overhead | Ongoing business expenses not directly tied to producing a product (e.g., rent, utilities) |
| liability | A financial obligation or debt owed by a company |
| asset | Anything of value owned by a company (property, equipment, IP) |
| equity | The value of ownership in a company after debts are subtracted |
| break-even | The point where revenue equals costs — no profit, no loss |
| dividend | A share of company profits paid to shareholders |
| invoice | A document requesting payment for goods or services |
| budget | A financial plan allocating money across categories for a period |

---

## 3. Marketing & Sales

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| BOGOF | "Buy One Get One Free" — a sales promotion strategy |
| hook (noun) | Something that grabs attention or attracts customers |
| customer loyalty | The tendency of customers to keep returning to a brand |
| tangible | Something that can be physically touched or clearly measured |
| chart (verb) | To track and record progress or data over time |
| high-end | Premium, luxury, or expensive products aimed at affluent customers |
| brand equity | The value a brand name adds to a product |
| target market | The specific group of customers a business aims to reach |
| market share | The percentage of total sales in a market held by a company |
| USP | Unique Selling Proposition — what makes a product distinct from competitors |
| cold calling | Contacting potential customers by phone without prior arrangement |
| conversion rate | Percentage of potential customers who complete a purchase |
| endorsement | Public support of a product, often by a celebrity or expert |

---

## 4. Operations & Logistics

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| lead time | The time between placing an order and receiving it |
| child-proof | Designed to prevent children from opening or accessing |
| biodegradable | Capable of being broken down naturally by bacteria or other organisms |
| foolproof | So simple or robust that it cannot fail or be misused |
| packaging | The materials used to wrap or protect products |
| swap | To exchange one thing for another |
| supply chain | The entire network of production, logistics, and distribution for a product |
| outsourcing | Hiring an external company to perform services your business needs |
| procurement | The process of obtaining goods or services for business use |
| inventory | The stock of goods held by a company |
| quality control | Processes to ensure products meet required standards |
| scalability | A system's ability to grow and handle increased demand |

---

## 5. Negotiation & Communication

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| facilitate | To make a process easier or help it happen |
| resistance | Opposition to a change, idea, or action |
| tentative | Not definite or confirmed; provisional |
| counselling | Professional guidance given to help solve personal or work-related problems |
| settle | To reach an agreement; to resolve a dispute |
| persuasive | Able to convince others to agree or take action |
| mutually beneficial | Advantageous to all parties involved |
| mediation | A process where a neutral third party helps resolve a conflict |
| agenda | A list of items to be discussed in a meeting |
| minutes | An official written record of what was discussed in a meeting |
| consensus | General agreement among a group |
| counterpart | The person on the other side of a deal or negotiation |
| deadlock | A situation where no agreement can be reached |

---

## 6. Ethics & Corporate Responsibility

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| corporate social responsibility (CSR) | A company's commitment to ethical behavior and contributing positively to society |
| virtuous circle | A situation where positive actions repeatedly reinforce each other, leading to continuous improvement |
| ethics | Moral principles that guide behavior in business |
| transparency | Openness and clarity in business operations and communications |
| sustainability | Operating in ways that do not deplete resources or harm the environment long-term |
| compliance | Following laws, regulations, and internal company policies |
| conflict of interest | A situation where personal interests could improperly influence professional decisions |
| whistleblower | A person who reports illegal or unethical practices within an organization |
| code of conduct | A set of rules outlining proper professional behavior within a company |
| stakeholder | Anyone affected by a company's decisions (employees, customers, investors, community) |

---

## 7. HR & Organizational Behavior

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| ethnic minorities | Groups of people who differ in race or culture from the majority of a population |
| disabled | Having a physical or mental impairment that substantially limits major life activities |
| treadmill (figurative) | A situation where someone works hard but makes no real progress |
| cope with | To deal with a difficult situation effectively |
| frustration | The feeling of being upset because something is not working as expected |
| posse | Informal: a group of supporters or associates |
| resident | A person who lives in a particular place; also an employee based at a specific location |
| burnout | A state of physical and emotional exhaustion from prolonged workplace stress |
| morale | The confidence, enthusiasm, and discipline of a team at work |
| incentive | Something that motivates employees to work harder or achieve goals |
| probation | A trial period for new employees to demonstrate their suitability |
| redundancy | When an employee's job is eliminated, often due to restructuring |

---

## 8. Risk & Safety

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| hazardous | Involving risk or danger, especially to health or safety |
| risk | The possibility that something harmful or undesirable will occur |
| sterile | Free from bacteria or other microorganisms; also means clean/safe |
| minimum | The smallest acceptable amount or level |
| tightly | In a secure, firm manner; with no slack |
| resistant | Not affected by; able to withstand something |
| triggers (noun) | Things that cause a reaction or set off a problem |
| contingency plan | A backup plan in case the main plan fails |
| mitigation | Actions taken to reduce the severity of a risk |
| audit | A formal inspection or review of a company's accounts or processes |

---

## 9. Useful Phrases & Collocations

| Phrase | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| under pressure | Experiencing stress from external demands or expectations |
| at risk | In danger; exposed to possible harm or loss |
| in regard to / with regard to | Concerning; in relation to |
| decision-making | The process of choosing between options |
| organizational structure | The system of roles and responsibilities within a company |
| particularly problematic | Especially difficult or causing specific issues |
| keep costs to a minimum | To limit spending as much as possible |
| builds customer loyalty | Creates repeated, committed customer relationships |

---

*Study tip: Don't just memorize definitions. For each term, write one sentence using it in a realistic business context. That's what the exam actually tests.*
